My ideal job is to become a cybersecurity architect.
The role of the architect is to utilise their abundance of knowledge and experience to create and implement security solutions for organisations.
In addition to designing the architecture, they will also be responsible for maintaining defence, responding to incidents and provide guidance with
risk assessments and security policies. (Cyber Security Education & Cyber Degrees, 2019)

This job appeals to me because I have aspirations to become a cybersecurity specialist, but why stop there? The sky is the limit and I want to push
myself to that limit and achieve something that I once thought was impossible. Becoming a leader and providing guidance to a team of specialists would be a
massive achievement in my career.

To become a cybersecurity architect, there is a long and arduous task ahead. First and foremost, is attaining a tertiary degree such as a
Bachelor or Master’s degree in an IT or related field such as cybersecurity or computer science. (Cyber Security Education & Cyber Degrees, 2019)
It is also expected for potential candidates to have done other security certifications such as CISSP, CISM, SABSA, CRISC, SANS, CCNP, etc. (Cyber Degrees, 2019)
Aside from degrees and certifications, security architect applicants are also expected to have a good amount of experience working in the field.

My current skills, qualifications and experience are very little.
I come from a non-IT based background being a car mechanic, so I have achieved a Certificate III level qualification thus far.
I am currently studying a Certificate IV in Cyber Security as my first step towards a career in the cybersecurity field.
My plan to achieve a career as a cybersecurity architect would start with finishing off my Cert IV, continue with the Bachelor of Information Technology and move into a Bachelor of Cyber Security.
I would like to change jobs during my studies to gain precious experience in the IT field, hopefully as a security analyst or penetration tester.
Once I have finished my Bachelor studies, I would like to continue to attaining a Master’s degree and continue to develop my skill set and knowledge while working in a relevant job.
My hope is once I have completed my studies, and attained certifications such as CCNP, CISSP and SANS,
I would have advanced in my career to the point where I can show employers the wealth of experience I possess and the drive I have to become an architect.
