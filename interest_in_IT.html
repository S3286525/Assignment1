I have always had a strong interest in PCs from a young age. I remember when I first saw my cousin's PC when I was about
six years old and how amazed I was at how you could print an image on the screen to paper!
I am very passionate about computer hardware, I build PCs as a hobby and I enjoy overclocking my PC in my spare time to see how far I can push the parts.

In terms of experience though, is where I fall short. I have been working as a car mechanic for almost 10 years, so IT is merely a hobby.
This all changed when I saw the free TAFE courses from the government and I thought to myself, maybe it's time for a change!
I enrolled in a Certificate IV in Cyber Security to take the first steps into, I hope, a new and challenging career.
During my time studying the Cert IV, I have thoroughly enjoyed learning about computer networking, security principles and programming
with Python.

I chose RMIT to do my studies because I am working full-time and raising a young family.
I work 38 hours per week at minimum and also attend two-night classes for my Certificate IV so attending university via
face to face method was not an option.
In my current circumstances, RMIT, in-collaboration with Open Universities Australia (OUA) catered to my needs of an online study
option.
I don't have any previous tertiary study so getting a direct entry into a Bachelor degree is almost an impossible task, let alone
finding one that was also available to study online.
After speaking to OUA and RMIT, they suggested for me to do the 'Introduction to IT' and 'Introduction to Programming' subjects
as a stepping stone towards completing a Bachelor degree while getting a taste of what university will be like.

I expect to come out of the two subjects with much more in-depth knowledge of the IT field.
I will improve on thinking outside the box and apply my knowledge of IT to help improve the quality of life of people and other
industries.
I would like to improve my critical thinking as well as my creative imagination and I believe this course will help me achieve that.
I hope to learn more about cybersecurity and the ways we can improve the protection of confidential and sensitive data, catching
threats before they do damage and progress into a career in cyberspace.










Daniel Lay, 2019
